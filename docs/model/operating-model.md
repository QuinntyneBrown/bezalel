# Operating model

How the proposed Bezalel model sits within FaithTech Toronto and could operate in practice.

## Relationship to FaithTech Toronto

Bezalel is not a consultancy, agency, separate legal entity, or established delivery organization. It is the working name for a FaithTech Toronto team exploring whether a community can combine the product and engineering discipline associated with ThoughtWorks with a redemptive, free-to-mission-partner model.

FaithTech Toronto provides the community, Create programs, hackathon infrastructure, and relationships with practitioners and mission partners. The Bezalel model proposes a path for selected hackathon work to continue toward durable software when the problem is a strong fit, a capable team is available, and backing partners can supply the funding or in-kind resources needed. The ThoughtWorks analogy applies to craft and delivery discipline, not fees, clients, or corporate structure.

Concretely:

- FaithTech Toronto convenes Create and hackathons, where practitioners are formed and mission-partner problems are explored.
- The team tests a continuation path of discovery, selection, scoping, building, and handoff for promising prototypes.
- Mission partners receive the work without a service fee and contribute domain knowledge, time, feedback, and ownership of the resulting system.
- Backing partners contribute funding, tools, mentorship, professional services, or other resources without buying control of the project pipeline.
- Practitioners move through one connected community rather than joining a separate consulting company.

## How the experiment is intended to run

- Continuation projects are staffed as small teams (see [`docs/people/roles.md`](../people/roles.md)) drawn from the practitioner community.
- Each project has a named lead accountable for delivery and a domain steward representing the mission partner.
- Work is scoped, timeboxed, and tracked against explicit outcomes — see [`docs/playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md`](../playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md).
- No project begins without enough committed practitioner capacity and backing to make its scope credible. Free does not mean open-ended or on demand.
- Projects are recorded in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/) regardless of outcome, so the team can learn whether the model works.
