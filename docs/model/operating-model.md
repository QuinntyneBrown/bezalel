# Operating model

How Bezalel runs, and how it relates to FaithTech Toronto.

## Relationship to FaithTech Toronto

Bezalel operates through FaithTech Toronto rather than as a separate legal or organizational entity. FaithTech Toronto provides the community, the Create formation program, the hackathon infrastructure, and the pipeline of practitioners and partners. Bezalel is the specific function within that ecosystem responsible for taking validated prototypes to durable, production-quality software.

Concretely:

- FaithTech Toronto owns Create and the hackathon calendar.
- Bezalel owns the engagement process: discovery, scoping, delivery, and handoff once a prototype has been selected to move forward.
- The two share a practitioner community — most Bezalel engagement staff came up through Create or a hackathon.

## How Bezalel runs day to day

- Engagements are staffed as small teams (see [`docs/people/roles.md`](../people/roles.md)) drawn from the practitioner community.
- Each engagement has a named lead accountable for delivery and a domain steward representing the partner.
- Work is scoped, timeboxed, and tracked against explicit outputs — see [`docs/playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md`](../playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md).
- Engagements are recorded in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/) regardless of outcome, so the model improves over time.
