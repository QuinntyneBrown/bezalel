# Offerings

Bezalel's service catalog. Each offering states what it is and when it fits.

## Discovery

**What it is:** a structured process of understanding a partner's problem, constraints, users, and success criteria, before any commitment to build anything.

**When it fits:** always, before scoping an engagement — including for prototypes that came out of a hackathon. A hackathon demo tells you a team could build *something*; it does not tell you what the partner actually needs sustained.

See [`docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md`](../playbooks/running-a-discovery.md).

## Prototyping

**What it is:** fast, disposable exploration of a solution approach — typically what happens at a hackathon, but occasionally run as a standalone Bezalel activity when a partner problem needs de-risking before a full engagement is scoped.

**When it fits:** early, when it's unclear whether a technical approach is even viable, or when the problem itself needs to be validated with real users before committing engineering time.

## Architecture review

**What it is:** an experienced practitioner or small team reviews an existing system (a partner's current tooling, or a hackathon prototype being considered for an engagement) and produces a written assessment of risks, gaps, and a recommended path forward.

**When it fits:** before scoping an engagement around an existing prototype, or when a partner already has some technology and needs an outside, competent opinion before investing further.

## Build

**What it is:** the core Bezalel engagement — a staffed team takes a validated problem (and often a validated prototype) and builds durable, tested, documented, production-quality software, with an explicit handoff plan.

**When it fits:** after discovery and (if relevant) architecture review have validated the problem and approach, and the partner is committed to operating the resulting system.

## Technical coaching

**What it is:** ongoing, lighter-touch support for a partner's own staff or volunteers — code review, architecture guidance, incident support — without Bezalel owning delivery.

**When it fits:** after a build engagement closes, to help a partner's team sustain what was delivered, or in cases where a partner has in-house technical capacity that just needs leveling up rather than a full build team.
