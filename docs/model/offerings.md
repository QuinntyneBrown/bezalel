# Program activities

The activities FaithTech Toronto is exploring through Bezalel. This is not a service catalog: the activities are not sold separately, Bezalel is not available for hire, and continuation work depends on mission fit, capacity, and backing.

## Create

**What it is:** FaithTech's formation environment, where practitioners grow in community and real problems from churches, ministries, and nonprofits can surface.

**Role in the model:** develops the people and partner relationships that make redemptive building possible. See [`docs/playbooks/create-night-format.md`](../playbooks/create-night-format.md).

## Hackathon prototyping

**What it is:** fast, time-boxed exploration of a mission partner's problem and a possible technical response.

**Role in the model:** tests problem-team fit and technical direction. A prototype is evidence, not a finished product or a promise that work will continue. See [`docs/playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md`](../playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md).

## Discovery and selection

**What it is:** structured work to understand the problem, users, constraints, redemptive aim, mission-partner commitment, technical risks, and realistic path to sustainability.

**Role in the model:** determines whether a hackathon project should stop, pause, or continue. Selection also requires a capable team and enough backing; it is not a sales process. See [`docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md`](../playbooks/running-a-discovery.md).

## Partner-backed continuation build

**What it is:** a small, multidisciplinary team takes a validated problem and, often, a hackathon prototype toward durable, tested, documented, production-quality software with an explicit handoff plan.

**Role in the model:** explores whether ThoughtWorks-level product and engineering discipline can be practiced redemptively in a community model. The mission partner is not charged a service fee; practitioner time and project costs are carried by volunteers and backing partners.

## Handoff and learning

**What it is:** documentation, training, technical coaching where capacity allows, outcome review, and a record of what the project taught the team.

**Role in the model:** helps the mission partner operate the result without dependency on Bezalel and feeds evidence back into Create, future hackathons, and the model itself.
