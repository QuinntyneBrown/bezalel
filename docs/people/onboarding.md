# Onboarding

How a new person plugs into Bezalel.

## If you're a technologist new to the community

1. Start at Create — this is the front door for practitioners, not a direct project assignment. See [`docs/playbooks/create-night-format.md`](../playbooks/create-night-format.md).
2. Read [`MANIFESTO.md`](../../MANIFESTO.md) and [`docs/vision/redemptive-frame.md`](../vision/redemptive-frame.md) so you understand the point of view before you commit time.
3. Join a hackathon when one is scheduled — this is the fastest way to experience the model firsthand and meet people testing continuation projects.
4. If a hackathon prototype you worked on is selected and backed to continue, you may be invited to stay on through discovery and into a project — see [`docs/playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md`](../playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md).

## If you're joining an active continuation project

1. Read the project's record in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/) in full before your first working session.
2. Understand your role and its commitment level in [`docs/people/roles.md`](roles.md), and be honest with the lead about your real availability.
3. Review [`docs/model/guardrails.md`](../model/guardrails.md) so you know the lines the project is expected to hold.
4. Meet the domain steward early — you're building for their reality, not your assumptions about it.

## If you're a partner (church, ministry, or nonprofit)

1. Bring a real, specific problem into the FaithTech Toronto Create and hackathon community, not a request to hire Bezalel for generic technology — see [`docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md`](../playbooks/running-a-discovery.md) for what a good problem statement looks like.
2. Expect prototyping and discovery before any commitment to continue. Bezalel is not a consultancy, and free participation does not guarantee that a project will be selected or resourced.
3. Identify a domain steward on your side who can stay involved throughout. Your organization will not be charged a service fee, but its time, feedback, and ownership are essential.

## If you're a potential backing partner

1. Read the operating model and guardrails to understand that backing enables the whole community; it does not buy a consulting deliverable or priority in the project pipeline.
2. Be concrete about what you can contribute: funding, practitioner capacity, tools, venues, professional services, mentorship, or another resource.
3. Agree up front on attribution, constraints, and conflicts so the mission partner's interests remain primary.
