# Onboarding

How a new person plugs into Bezalel.

## If you're a technologist new to the community

1. Start at Create — this is the front door for practitioners, not a direct engagement assignment. See [`docs/playbooks/create-night-format.md`](../playbooks/create-night-format.md).
2. Read [`MANIFESTO.md`](../../MANIFESTO.md) and [`docs/vision/redemptive-frame.md`](../vision/redemptive-frame.md) so you understand the point of view before you commit time.
3. Join a hackathon when one is scheduled — this is the fastest way to experience the model firsthand and meet the people running engagements.
4. If a hackathon prototype you worked on is selected to continue, you may be invited to stay on through discovery and into an engagement — see [`docs/playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md`](../playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md).

## If you're joining an active engagement directly

1. Read the engagement's record in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/) in full before your first working session.
2. Understand your role and its commitment level in [`docs/people/roles.md`](roles.md), and be honest with the lead about your real availability.
3. Review [`docs/model/guardrails.md`](../model/guardrails.md) so you know the lines the engagement is expected to hold.
4. Meet the domain steward early — you're building for their reality, not your assumptions about it.

## If you're a partner (church, ministry, or nonprofit)

1. Bring a real, specific problem, not a request for generic technology — see [`docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md`](../playbooks/running-a-discovery.md) for what a good problem statement looks like.
2. Expect discovery before any commitment to build — this protects both you and the practitioners.
3. Identify a domain steward on your side who can stay engaged throughout.
