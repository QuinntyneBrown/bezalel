# Roles

Who does what across the proposed Bezalel flywheel, and the commitment level each role implies.

## Practitioner

**Responsibilities:** contributes hands-on skilled work — engineering, design, product, QA — to Create, a hackathon, or a continuation project.

**Commitment level:** variable. Can be a single Create night, a hackathon weekend, or a multi-week project commitment. Practitioners should be honest about their real availability when joining a project team, since under-delivery there affects a mission partner, not just a project.

## Mentor

**Responsibilities:** supports the formation of newer practitioners during Create and hackathons; may also provide architecture guidance during a continuation project without being on the build team day to day.

**Commitment level:** ongoing but light-touch — typically a recurring but bounded time commitment (e.g., a few hours per Create cycle).

## Domain steward

**Responsibilities:** represents the mission partner's interests and real domain knowledge throughout discovery and a continuation project. Validates that what's being built actually addresses the real problem, not an assumed one. Usually a mission-partner staff member or leader, not a Bezalel practitioner.

**Commitment level:** engaged throughout discovery and the project lifecycle — regular check-ins, availability for questions, sign-off at key milestones.

## Lead

**Responsibilities:** accountable for a continuation project's delivery: scoping, staffing, timeline, technical decisions, backing plan, and execution of the handoff. The lead is the single point of accountability the domain steward and practitioners can rely on.

**Commitment level:** high and sustained for the duration of the project — this is not a light-touch role.

## Backing partner

**Responsibilities:** contributes funding, tools, venues, professional services, mentorship, or other resources that let the team serve a mission partner without charging a fee. Respects the mission partner's ownership and the selection guardrails rather than treating support as a purchase order.

**Commitment level:** defined for each project or program cycle, with the contribution and any constraints documented before work begins.
