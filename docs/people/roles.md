# Roles

Who does what across the Bezalel flywheel, and the commitment level each role implies.

## Practitioner

**Responsibilities:** contributes hands-on skilled work — engineering, design, product, QA — to Create, a hackathon, or a Bezalel engagement.

**Commitment level:** variable. Can be a single Create night, a hackathon weekend, or a multi-week engagement commitment. Practitioners should be honest about their real availability when joining an engagement team, since under-delivery there affects a partner, not just a project.

## Mentor

**Responsibilities:** supports the formation of newer practitioners during Create and hackathons; may also provide architecture guidance during an engagement without being on the delivery team day to day.

**Commitment level:** ongoing but light-touch — typically a recurring but bounded time commitment (e.g., a few hours per Create cycle).

## Domain steward

**Responsibilities:** represents the partner's interests and real domain knowledge throughout discovery and an engagement. Validates that what's being built actually addresses the real problem, not an assumed one. Usually a partner staff member or leader, not a Bezalel practitioner.

**Commitment level:** engaged throughout discovery and the engagement lifecycle — regular check-ins, availability for questions, sign-off at key milestones.

## Lead

**Responsibilities:** accountable for an engagement's delivery: scoping, staffing, timeline, technical decisions, and ensuring the handoff plan is actually executed. The lead is the single point of accountability the domain steward and practitioners can rely on.

**Commitment level:** high and sustained for the duration of the engagement — this is not a light-touch role.
