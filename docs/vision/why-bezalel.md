# Why Bezalel

The name, the calling, and the specific gap Bezalel fills.

## The name

Bezalel is named after the craftsman in Exodus 31:1–5, the first person Scripture describes as filled with the Spirit of God — not for prophecy or leadership, but for skilled making: "wisdom, understanding, knowledge, and all kinds of skill," to design and build with gold, silver, bronze, stone, and wood.

The thesis this points to: redemptive work can be expressed through skilled craft, not only through explicitly "spiritual" activity. Building well — with real engineering discipline — is itself a legitimate expression of the mission. The FaithTech Toronto team does not need to turn that work into a consultancy to take the craft seriously.

## The gap Bezalel fills

Three things already exist in this space, and none of them alone solves the problem:

- **A normal dev shop** can build well-architected software, but has no pipeline of mission-aligned problems, no formation process for practitioners who want their skill to matter, and usually depends on a client relationship that prices many mission partners out.
- **A one-off hackathon** surfaces energy, real problems, and fast prototypes, but by design does not produce durable software. Most hackathon projects die within weeks because there is no path from "it demoed well" to "it is deployed, documented, and maintained."
- **An informal volunteer tech effort** has goodwill and proximity to the problem, but may lack the engineering rigor, availability, backing, and continuity to ship and sustain real systems — leading to the fragile, unmaintained software described in [`MANIFESTO.md`](../../MANIFESTO.md).

Bezalel is the working hypothesis FaithTech Toronto is testing against that gap: Create forms practitioners and surfaces problems, hackathons test solution ideas, and selected projects continue through partner-backed teams at no charge to the mission partner. The "ThoughtWorks" comparison names the hoped-for level of multidisciplinary product and engineering craft, not a commercial relationship.

The model is not yet proven. The aim of this repository is to make the hypothesis concrete enough to test, record what happens, and change it in response to evidence. See [`docs/model/the-pipeline.md`](../model/the-pipeline.md) for the proposed hand-offs.
