# Hackathon to continuation project

How a hackathon prototype becomes (or doesn't become) a free-to-mission-partner, partner-backed continuation project.

## Purpose

Give a clear, honest process for deciding which hackathon prototypes deserve continued investment, and prevent every demo-day project from being assumed to deserve one.

## When to use

Within a few weeks after a hackathon, while the prototype, team, and partner relationship are still fresh.

## Steps

1. **List candidate prototypes.** Not every team's output is a candidate — start with ones that visibly worked and addressed a real, specific partner problem.
2. **Re-confirm partner interest.** Demo-day enthusiasm is not the same as sustained interest. Go back to the partner after the event and ask if the problem still matters and whether they want to continue.
3. **Assess technical viability honestly.** A hackathon prototype is built under time pressure with shortcuts. Have an experienced practitioner who wasn't on the team assess what it would actually take to make it durable — this is often very different from "just polish it."
4. **Check for a willing continuity link.** At least one practitioner from the hackathon team should be willing to stay involved through discovery, to carry context forward.
5. **Run discovery.** Do not skip this step because a prototype exists — see [`docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md`](running-a-discovery.md). The prototype is a hypothesis about the solution, not a validated one.
6. **Identify the backing required.** Estimate the practitioner time, funding, tools, mentorship, and in-kind support a responsible continuation would need. This is a resourcing check, not a price quoted to the mission partner.
7. **Decide.** If the problem, mission-partner commitment, technical path, team capacity, and credible backing all check out, proceed to [`docs/playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md`](scoping-an-engagement.md). If not, it's fine — even good — to let a prototype end at the hackathon. Record the decision either way.

## Outputs

- A short written assessment per candidate prototype: continue, don't continue, or not yet
- For prototypes that continue: a discovery in progress
- A brief note in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/) even for prototypes that don't continue, so the pattern of what does and doesn't move forward is visible over time
