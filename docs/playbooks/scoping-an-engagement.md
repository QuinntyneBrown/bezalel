# Scoping a partner project

How to turn a validated discovery into a scoped, staffed, partner-backed continuation project.

## Purpose

Convert a validated problem and redemptive angle into a concrete, time-boxed plan with a team, backing, a scope, and success criteria everyone has agreed to — before continuation code is written.

## When to use

After a hackathon prototype completes discovery with a "continue" recommendation. A recommendation is not yet a commitment: the project must also have the team capacity and backing needed to deliver responsibly.

## Steps

1. **Write the one-page project brief.** Use [`docs/engagements/_template.md`](../engagements/_template.md): problem, mission partner, redemptive angle, scope, team, backing, and status.
2. **Define the minimum durable scope.** Distinguish what must exist for the partner to be genuinely better off and able to maintain the system, from what would be nice but can wait. Resist scope creep toward an idealized version that never ships.
3. **Staff the team.** Assign a lead and confirm a domain steward on the partner side — see [`docs/people/roles.md`](../people/roles.md). Match practitioner skill and availability honestly against the scope; don't overcommit volunteer time.
4. **Confirm backing.** Identify the volunteer time, funding, tools, mentorship, and in-kind support the project needs. Do not start on the assumption that goodwill will fill an unresourced gap later.
5. **Set a timeline with checkpoints.** Continuation projects should be timeboxed with visible milestones, not open-ended.
6. **Check against guardrails.** Confirm the project doesn't cross any line in [`docs/model/guardrails.md`](../model/guardrails.md) before committing.
7. **Agree on the handoff plan up front.** Decide, before building starts, how the mission partner will operate and maintain the system after the project ends — training, documentation, and any resourced ongoing support.
8. **Get mission-partner sign-off.** The mission partner should see and agree to the brief, especially the scope and success criteria, before work begins. Their participation is required, but they are not being sold a service or charged a fee.

## Outputs

- A completed project record in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/), status set to active
- A staffed team with a named lead and domain steward
- Confirmed backing and an agreed scope, timeline, and handoff plan
