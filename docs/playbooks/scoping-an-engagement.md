# Scoping an engagement

How to turn a validated discovery into a scoped, staffed Bezalel engagement.

## Purpose

Convert a validated problem and redemptive angle into a concrete, time-boxed plan with a team, a scope, and success criteria everyone has agreed to — before any code is written.

## When to use

Immediately after a discovery reaches a "go" recommendation, whether the discovery originated from a hackathon prototype or a direct partner request.

## Steps

1. **Write the one-page engagement brief.** Use [`docs/engagements/_template.md`](../engagements/_template.md): problem, partner, redemptive angle, scope, team, status.
2. **Define the minimum durable scope.** Distinguish what must exist for the partner to be genuinely better off and able to maintain the system, from what would be nice but can wait. Resist scope creep toward an idealized version that never ships.
3. **Staff the team.** Assign a lead and confirm a domain steward on the partner side — see [`docs/people/roles.md`](../people/roles.md). Match practitioner skill and availability honestly against the scope; don't overcommit volunteer time.
4. **Set a timeline with checkpoints.** Bezalel engagements should be timeboxed with visible milestones, not open-ended.
5. **Check against guardrails.** Confirm the engagement doesn't cross any line in [`docs/model/guardrails.md`](../model/guardrails.md) before committing.
6. **Agree on the handoff plan up front.** Decide, before building starts, how the partner will operate and maintain the system after the engagement ends — training, documentation, ongoing support arrangement.
7. **Get partner sign-off.** The partner should see and agree to the brief, especially the scope and success criteria, before work begins.

## Outputs

- A completed engagement record in [`docs/engagements/`](../engagements/), status set to active
- A staffed team with a named lead and domain steward
- An agreed scope, timeline, and handoff plan
