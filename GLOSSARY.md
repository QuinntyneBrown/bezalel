# Glossary

Shared vocabulary used across this repository. If a term used elsewhere in these docs is unclear, it should be defined here.

**Bezalel**
The working name for FaithTech Toronto's exploration of a free-to-mission-partner, partner-backed model for building redemptive technology with serious product and engineering discipline. Bezalel is not a consultancy or a separate organization.

**Redemptive technology**
Technology that actively repairs, restores, or dignifies something broken, neglected, or excluded — distinct from merely avoiding harm. See [`MANIFESTO.md`](MANIFESTO.md) and [`docs/vision/redemptive-frame.md`](docs/vision/redemptive-frame.md).

**Engagement / partner project**
A scoped, time-bounded continuation project in which a FaithTech Toronto project team takes a validated problem, often represented by a hackathon prototype, toward durable and well-architected software. "Engagement" is an internal project term, not a consulting contract: the mission partner is not charged for the work. See [`docs/engagements/_template.md`](docs/engagements/_template.md) and [`docs/playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md`](docs/playbooks/scoping-an-engagement.md).

**Practitioner**
A technologist actively contributing skilled work — engineering, design, product, architecture — to Create, a hackathon, or a continuation project. See [`docs/people/roles.md`](docs/people/roles.md).

**Domain steward**
The person, often from the mission partner, who holds real knowledge of the problem domain and represents the mission partner's interests throughout a project. See [`docs/people/roles.md`](docs/people/roles.md).

**Create**
FaithTech's ongoing formation program for technologists — the entry point of the flywheel, where practitioners are developed and real problems from churches, ministries, and nonprofits are surfaced. See [`docs/playbooks/create-night-format.md`](docs/playbooks/create-night-format.md).

**Hackathon**
A compressed, time-boxed event where teams prototype solutions against problems surfaced through Create. Hackathons produce candidate prototypes, not finished products. See [`docs/playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md`](docs/playbooks/hackathon-to-engagement.md).

**The flywheel**
The proposed operating loop: Create → hackathon → partner-backed build → feedback into Create. See [`docs/model/the-pipeline.md`](docs/model/the-pipeline.md).

**Mission partner**
The church, ministry, or mission-driven nonprofit that owns the problem being solved and receives the resulting technology without paying a service fee. Unless qualified otherwise, "partner" means mission partner in this repository.

**Backing partner**
A person or organization that helps make partner projects possible through funding, tools, venues, professional services, mentorship, or other resources. Backing does not turn a mission partner into a client or entitle a backer to buy priority in the project pipeline.

**Discovery**
The structured process of understanding a mission partner's problem, constraints, and success criteria before deciding whether to scope a continuation project. See [`docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md`](docs/playbooks/running-a-discovery.md).
